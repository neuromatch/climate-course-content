            known_hash=None,

        )

    return file

Section 1: El NiÃ±o-Southern Oscillation (ENSO)#

As you learned in Day 1 and 2, one of the most significant large-scale climate variabilities is El NiÃ±o-Southern Oscillation (ENSO). ENSO can change the global atmospheric circulation, which in turn, influences temperature and precipitation across the globe.

Despite being a single climate phenomenon, ENSO exhibits three distinct phases:

  • El NiÃ±o: A warming of the ocean surface, or above-average sea surface temperatures, in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.

  • La NiÃ±a: A cooling of the ocean surface, or below-average sea surface temperatures, in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.

  • Neutral: Neither El NiÃ±o or La NiÃ±a. Often tropical Pacific SSTs are generally close to average.

In Day 2, you practiced utilizing a variety of Xarray tools to examine variations in sea surface temperature (SST) during El NiÃ±o and La NiÃ±a events by calculating the Oceanic NiÃ±o Index (ONI) from reanalysis data over the time period 2000-2014.

In contrast to previous days, in this tutorial, you will use satellite-based SST data to monitor ENSO over a longer time period starting in 1981.

Section 1.1: Calculate SST Anomaly#

Optimum Interpolation Sea Surface Temperature (OISST) is a long-term Climate Data Record that incorporates observations from different platforms (satellites, ships, buoys, and Argo floats) into a regular global grid. OISST data is originally produced at daily and 1/4Â° spatial resolution. To avoid the large amount of data processing of daily data, we use the monthly aggregated OISST SST data provided by NOAA Physical Systems Laboratory.

# download the monthly sea surface temperature data from NOAA Physical System

# from the NOAA CDR program.

The different phases of ENSO are nominally defined based on a threshold of \(\pm\) 0.5 with the ONI index.

  • El NiÃ±o [ONI values higher than 0.5]: surface waters in the east-central tropical Pacific are at least 0.5 degrees Celsius warmer than normal.

  • La NiÃ±a [ONI values lower than -0.5]: surface waters in the west tropical Pacific are at least 0.5 degrees Celsius cooler than normal.

The neutral phase is when ONI values are in between these two thresholds. Letâ€™s create the ONI plot that is used by NOAA and other organizations to monitor ENSO phases.

# set up the plot size
fig, ax = plt.subplots()

# create the filled area when ONI values are above 0.5 for El Nino
ax.fill_between(
    oni.time.data,
    oni.where(oni >= 0.5).data,
    0.5,
    color="red",
    alpha=0.9,
)

# create the filled area when ONI values are below -0.5 for La Nina
ax.fill_between(
    oni.time.data,
    oni.where(oni <= -0.5).data,
    -0.5,
    color="blue",
    alpha=0.9,
)

# create the time series of ONI
oni.plot(color="black", ax=ax)

# add the threshold lines on the plot
ax.axhline(0, color="black", lw=0.5)
ax.axhline(0.5, color="red", linewidth=0.5, linestyle="dotted")
ax.axhline(-0.5, color="blue", linewidth=0.5, linestyle="dotted")
# aesthetics
ax.set_title("Oceanic NiÃ±o Index")
ax.set_xlabel('Time (months)')
ax.set_ylabel('ONI')
